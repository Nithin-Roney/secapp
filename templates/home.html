Cybersecurity safeguards data, privacy, and systems from cyber threats, prevents financial losses, ensures secure transactions, and preserves digital trust.

01.

“Privacy is not an option, and it shouldn’t be the price we accept for just getting on the Internet.” – Gary Kovacs

Many online services, apps, and platforms collect vast amounts of personal data—such as browsing habits, location, and preferences—often without users fully understanding how it will be used. Companies then use this data for targeted ads, analytics, or even sell it to third parties. Kovacs is emphasizing that people should not have to choose between using the internet and protecting their privacy. Instead, privacy protections should be built into digital services by default. It’s a call for greater transparency, stronger privacy laws, and user control over personal data.

02.

“Cybersecurity is not just about protecting your devices. It’s about protecting yourself.”– Anonymous

While many people think of cybersecurity as protecting their computers, phones, or networks, it actually goes much deeper—it’s about protecting your identity, privacy, financial information, and even your personal safety in the digital world. Good cybersecurity practices—like strong passwords, two-factor authentication, avoiding phishing scams, and using secure connections—aren’t just about securing devices; they help protect you as a person in the digital age.

03.

“There’s no silver bullet solution with cyber security, a layered defense is the only viable defense.” – Scott

James Scott emphasizes that cybersecurity requires multiple layers of protection rather than relying on a single tool or solution. In other words, there is no "silver bullet"—no single technology or approach that can guarantee complete security. A layered defense (also known as defense in depth) means using a combination of security measures to protect systems, networks, and data. This approach ensures that even if one layer is breached, other layers remain to stop or mitigate the attack.
